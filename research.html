Research

DFT study of Potential Molecules as Inhibitors for Metallic Surface in Neutral Systems.

The study of metallic surfaces is extremely important due to their significant impact on the global economy, where processes such as catalysis and corrosion play crucial roles. In particular, corrosion stands out as a relevant problem, making the development of effective anti-corrosion materials urgent. However, studies on anticorrosives that act in neutral pH environments are scarce, as most research has been focused on acidic and alkaline environments. Therefore, our group performs simulations using Density Functional Theory, with the aim of identifying potential candidates capable of acting as anticorrosive agents on metallic surfaces in a neutral environment.
